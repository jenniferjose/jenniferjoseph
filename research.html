Role of different interactions involved in the biomolecular condensation of α-synuclein protein

Computational study of interactions involving the bio-molecular condensate formation of α-synuclein protein: The research involved studying the different types of interactions that occur during bio-molecular condensate formation using coarse-grained polymer models. Langevin Dynamics simulations using LAMMPS was performed to investigate the phase-separating behavior of proteins under varying ionic strengths of interactions for α-synuclein.

Computational study of breakage-dependent amyloid kinetics:


Click here to read more about this work

The work involved investigating the kinetics of breakage-dependent amyloid growth through kinetic Monte Carlo simulations. By developing a robust model, we gained valuable insights into the impact of various parameters on the breakage dependent self-assembly kinetics of proteins. This model has the potential to redesign PMCA experiments, allowing for precise back-calculation of seed concentration and early detection of diseases associated with amyloid formation. The findings of this research have been published in ACS Chem. Neuroscience.
