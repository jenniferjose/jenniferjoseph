About Me

Welcome to my personal webpage! I am Jennifer Joseph, a Computational Biophysics Ph.D. graduate from the Department of Biosciences and Bioengineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay. With a strong background in computational biophysics and a passion for leveraging cutting-edge technologies in drug design and therapeutic development, I am dedicated to unraveling the functional mechanisms of biomolecules in diseases.

During my doctoral studies, I focused on investigating protein-protein interactions leading to protein aggregates and biomolecular condensates. Through kinetic Monte Carlo simulations, I developed a robust model to understand breakage-dependent amyloid growth and its implications for disease detection. I also explored the formation of biomolecular condensates using Langevin Dynamics simulations, shedding light on the influence of electrostatic and hydrophobic interactions. Additionally, I contributed to a collaborative project on liquid-liquid phase separation, developing a predictive model based on surface properties.

Throughout my Ph.D., I expanded my skill set in programming languages, scientific software development, and molecular simulation techniques. My interdisciplinary background, combining computational expertise with wet lab experience, enables effective collaboration and understanding across diverse research domains.

Driven by a fascination for the intersection of drug design, artificial intelligence, and data science, I believe in the transformative power of integrating these disciplines to accelerate therapeutic development. Together, we can make substantial advancements in understanding the dynamics of drug molecules and their impact on target molecules, ultimately translating scientific discoveries into effective therapies for patients worldwide.

Please explore my webpage to learn more about my research, publications, and professional achievements. I am currently seeking opportunities in the United States and look forward to collaborating with like-minded individuals and organizations dedicated to advancing therapeutic interventions.

Thank you for visiting my webpage, and I invite you to contact me for any further information or collaboration opportunities.
